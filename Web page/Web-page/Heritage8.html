Lankathilaka Vihara Temple

Lankathilake Temple, situated in the picturesque town of Udunuwara, Sri Lanka, is a revered Buddhist temple that holds deep historical and cultural significance. Nestled amidst lush greenery and surrounded by the tranquil beauty of nature, the temple offers a serene and spiritual experience for visitors. The history of Lankathilake Temple dates back to the 14th century during the reign of King Bhuvanekabahu IV. It was built by the king's chief minister, Senalankadhikara, to commemorate his son's victory over the rebellious Gampola kingdom. The temple's name "Lankathilake" translates to "The Temple of the Resplendent Lanka," signifying its connection to the island nation. The temple's architecture is a splendid blend of Sinhalese and South Indian Dravidian styles. The main shrine, known as the "Viharageya," showcases exquisite stone carvings and intricate artwork that mesmerize visitors. The towering brick walls, ornate stone pillars, and meticulously crafted statues reflect the architectural brilliance of ancient Sri Lanka.

As you enter the temple, you are greeted by a striking stone staircase adorned with intricately carved guardian figures known as "dvarapalas." These imposing figures symbolize the protectors of the sacred space and create a sense of reverence and awe. The interior of Lankathilake Temple houses a magnificent seated Buddha statue, radiating serenity and tranquility. The walls are adorned with vibrant frescoes depicting scenes from Buddhist folklore and legends. These vivid paintings serve as a visual narrative, offering insights into the rich cultural heritage of the region. One of the highlights of the temple is the intricately carved moonstone at the entrance. This semi-circular slab, adorned with detailed patterns and symbols, represents the eternal cycle of life, emphasizing the Buddhist concept of impermanence.

The temple premises also encompass a beautiful bodhi tree, under which visitors can find solace and engage in meditation. The peaceful ambiance and the natural surroundings make it an ideal spot for spiritual reflection and contemplation. Lankathilake Temple in Udunuwara stands as a testament to Sri Lanka's rich Buddhist traditions and architectural legacy. Its historical significance, architectural grandeur, and serene ambiance make it a must-visit destination for those seeking spiritual enrichment and a deeper understanding of Sri Lanka's cultural heritage. Visiting this sacred site allows one to immerse oneself in the spiritual aura, witness the architectural brilliance of the past, and experience a sense of tranquility that rejuvenates the soul. Lankathilake Temple in Udunuwara truly encapsulates the beauty and essence of Sri Lanka's ancient cultural heritage.
