Bahiravakanda Vihara Buddha Statue

The Bahiravakanda Vihara Buddha Statue stands tall and majestic atop a hill in Kandy, Sri Lanka, offering a mesmerizing sight and a profound symbol of Buddhist spirituality. This iconic statue and the surrounding vihara (temple) hold great importance for both locals and visitors seeking a spiritual connection and panoramic views of the city. The history of the Bahiravokanda Vihara Buddha Statue dates back to the late 20th century. It was constructed in 1972 as a testament to the peaceful teachings of Buddhism and to honor Lord Buddha. The statue stands at an impressive height of 88 feet (26.8 meters) and is one of the largest Buddha statues in Sri Lanka. Ascending the hill to reach the statue is an experience in itself. Visitors can choose to climb the stairs or take a short drive to the entrance. As you make your way up, you'll be greeted with breathtaking vistas of Kandy, offering a panoramic view of the cityscape and surrounding natural beauty.

Upon reaching the statue, you will be awestruck by its grandeur and serene presence. The Buddha statue is crafted in white granite and depicts the sitting Buddha in the Dhyana Mudra, the meditative posture symbolizing inner peace and enlightenment. The gentle smile and serene expression of the Buddha radiate a sense of tranquility and harmony. Surrounding the statue, the vihara provides a space for devotees and visitors to pay their respects and engage in religious rituals. It is adorned with intricate paintings and murals depicting scenes from Buddhist mythology and teachings. The Bahiravokanda Vihara Buddha Statue serves as a sacred site for worship and meditation. Devotees and visitors often offer flowers, light oil lamps, and engage in silent reflection in the presence of the statue, seeking solace and spiritual upliftment. Beyond its religious significance, the site offers a captivating viewpoint. From the hilltop, one can admire the picturesque beauty of Kandy, with its rolling hills, lush greenery, and the glistening waters of the Mahaweli River. A visit to the Bahiravokanda Vihara Buddha Statue is a profound experience that combines spirituality, natural beauty, and cultural appreciation. It offers a chance to connect with the teachings of Buddhism, find inner peace, and marvel at the architectural and artistic splendor of this magnificent statue.
